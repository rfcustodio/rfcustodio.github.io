Biography

Ricardo Custódio holds a PhD in Computer Science (1999) from the Federal University of Rio Grande do Sul (UFRGS) and is a Full Professor at the Department of Informatics and Statistics of the Federal University of Santa Catarina (UFSC), where he coordinates the Computer Security Laboratory (LabSEC).

He works in the areas of information security and cryptography, with emphasis on digital signatures, verifiable credentials, public key infrastructure (PKI) and electronic identity management. His research interests also include cryptographic key management, theory and applications of finite fields, and quantum-resistant cryptography.

Current Research Lines

Digital Certification and Electronic Identity

Development of an integrated PKI and EI proposal, allowing users to have a useful, affordable and modern EI that makes use of on-demand PKI.

Electronic Document Security

New methods for digital signature of electronic documents, including post-quantum algorithms and improvements in the visual representation of digital signatures.

Post-Quantum Cryptography

Development of cryptographic algorithms resistant to quantum computer attacks.
